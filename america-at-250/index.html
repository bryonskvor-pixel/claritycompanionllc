United States of America · 1776 – 2026 · Semiquincentennial

The American
Argument.

For 250 years, America has been an argument between the promise it wrote down and the country it actually built. The words all men are created equal were true as an aspiration and false as a description, on the same day, in the same document. Everything since is the slow, contested, often reversed work of closing that gap: widening who counts as fully American, and sometimes narrowing it again.

The last word, for now

At 250, the argument is not over. The argument is the country.

The anniversary itself is being fought over in real time, by rival celebrations and rival stories. That is not a sign the experiment failed. It is the experiment. A nation founded on a promise it had not yet kept was always going to spend its life arguing about the distance left to travel.
